NETHERLANDS

MAIN ATTRACTION

The Netherlands is a small state in western Europe, characterised by its lowland relief and a continental climate tempered by the influences of the Atlantic Ocean. Historically, the country was a colony of the House of Habsburg, kings of Spain, and fought for its independence between the 16th and 17th centuries.

GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT THE COUNTRY

Geography and Demographics

The Netherlands is a nation located in northwestern Europe, bordered by the North Sea to the north and west, Belgium to the south and Germany to the east. In addition, the country includes island territories in the Caribbean such as Bonaire, Saba and Sint Eustatius. It is one of the most densely populated countries in the world, with much of its territory consisting of lowlands. The capital is Amsterdam, although the administration of the Kingdom of the Netherlands is located in The Hague.

Economy and Development

The Netherlands is one of the most developed states, classified as "high-income" by the World Bank. In 2018, it ranked tenth in the United Nations Human Development Index. It is also recognised for one of the lowest levels of perceived corruption and is considered one of the most consolidated democracies in the world.

Safety and Services

Although generally a safe country, it's important to be vigilant in large cities such as Amsterdam and Rotterdam, where bicycle theft and pickpocketing are common. Police, ambulance and fire services have a general emergency number 112. Port and airport security is handled by the Marechaussee, an independent gendarmerie. In most cities, there are municipal services with some police tasks.
